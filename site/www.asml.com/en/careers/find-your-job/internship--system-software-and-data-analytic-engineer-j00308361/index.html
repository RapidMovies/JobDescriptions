About the job

Introduction

ASML US, including its affiliates and subsidiaries, bring together the most creative minds in science and technology to develop lithography machines that are key to producing faster, cheaper, more energy-efficient microchips. We design, develop, integrate, market and service these advanced machines, which enable our customers - the world’s leading chipmakers - to reduce the size and increase the functionality of their microchips, which in turn leads to smaller, more powerful consumer electronics. Our headquarters are in Veldhoven, Netherlands, and we have 18 office locations around the United States including main offices in Chandler, Arizona, San Jose and San Diego, California, Wilton, Connecticut, and Hillsboro, Oregon.

Job Mission

The intern will collaborate closely with developers in D&E teams to create software that delivers advanced diagnostic capability and meets customer needs. The intern will be responsible for designing, coding and testing of key software modules throughout the development cycle. You will have an opportunity to work on software in support of ASML’s world leading e-Beam product line.

Job Description

  • The goal of the internship is for you to learn about working in a corporate setting and to provide your knowledge in solving real world problems in a team environment.
  • This internship is geared towards data analysis and programming :
    •
    • Gathering functional requirements by collaborating with multidisciplinary teams
    • Designing and Implementing the software
    • Executing the testing steps to qualify implementation
    • Intern will be required to document and update their progress as well as make an end of summer presentation to the group about what they achieved during their internship.

    Education and experience
    • Undergraduate or above in Computer Science, Electrical Engineering, or a Related Field
    • High proficiency in C#, C++ and Python is essential
    • Experience with data science technologies
    • Familiarity with OpenTelemetry tools
    • Ability to translate requirements, statistically analyze data, and effectively report problems and solutions through written and graphical formats
    • Familiarity with OOD principles

    Skills
    • Possess strong ownership characteristics and commitment to achieving results on time
    • Excellent written and oral communicative skills
    • Be a demonstrated “team player” with a quality orientation and interpersonal skills
    • Have strong problem solving skills and the ability to take initiative
    • Be willing to be “wrong” at times and be open to the ideas of others
